The Extraordinary Life of Katherine Johnson

  • 1918 - Katherine was born on August 26 1918.
  • 1929 - In 1929 Katherine started high school.
  • 1937 - In 1937 she graduated from West Virginia State University.
  • 1939 - In 1939 she was selected to be one of the first African American students to enroll at West Virginia University at Morgantown. In 1939 she also married James Goble.
  • 1953 - In 1953 she began work as a computer at NASA.
  • 1960 - In 1960 she co authored a paper on calculations for placing a spacecraft into orbit.
  • 1961 - In 1961 she calculated the path for Freedom 7.
  • 1962 - In 1962 John Glenn became the first American to orbit Earth. By that time, NASA had begun to use electronic computers. However, before Glenn left the ground, he wanted to make sure that the electronic computer had planned his flight correctly. He asked to have Johnson double check the computer’s calculations.
  • 1969 - Katherine was also part of the team that calculated where and when to launch the rocket for the Apollo 11 mission of 1969, which sent the first three men to the Moon.
  • 1986 - She retired from NASA in 1986.
  • 2015 - In 2015 she was awarded the U.S Presidential Medal of Freedom.
  • 2016 - In 2016 NASA honored her by naming a building after her. The building is called the Katherine G. Johnson computational research facility. In 2016 the book Hidden Figures: The American Dream and the Untold Story of the Black Women Mathematicians Who Helped Win the Space Race. The movie was also released.
